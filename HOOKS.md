# Hook Engineering · build hooks, do not just grade them

The first 1 to 2 seconds decide everything. v1 only checked "is there a hook" at the back. This stage CONSTRUCTS them at the front. For every surviving asset, write 3 to 5 hook options, then deliver the best as three layers.

## The three layers (every chosen post needs all three)
1. **On-screen text hook** · the words on frame one. This is what stops the scroll. 3 to 7 words.
2. **Verbal hook** · the first line she says out loud (if talking).
3. **Visual hook** · what is literally on screen in second one (the motion, the face, the reveal). A pretty static frame is not a visual hook.

## Hook frameworks (pick per clip, name the one used)
- **Open loop** · "The thing nobody warns you about catering a 200-person event..." (answered at the end → retention).
- **POV** · "POV: you're 30 days out from your first event and the mic just died."
- **Contrarian / "nobody tells you"** · "Nobody tells you the hardest part of a launch isn't the food."
- **Number / listicle** · "3 things I bought this week that are saving the event."
- **Watch-til-the-end** · payoff teased up front, delivered last frame.
- **Transformation** · before → after (empty room → plated table, raw clip → the moment).
- **Confessional / eavesdrop** · talking straight to camera like a voice note. Best for the authentic personal tier.
- **Timely / cultural** · ride a live moment (Fan Fest) the audience already cares about.

## Rules
- Match the framework to the footage, do not staple a listicle hook onto a quiet b-roll moment.
- On personal/TikTok, the confessional and POV hooks usually beat the polished ones (see RUBRIC authenticity premium).
- No em-dashes in any hook text.
- Every hook must be sayable/showable with the footage that actually exists. Do not write a hook for a shot she did not film, flag it as a shotlist item instead (Stage 8).
