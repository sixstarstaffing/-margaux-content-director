# Margaux · Content Knowledge Base (Sevyn method + Kailin's in-voice playbook)

This is the expertise layer. Distilled from Sevyn Buffins's "Social Club" course (the authority) and Kailin's own Content Playbook V2 (the applied, in-voice system). Use it to pick clips and write scroll-stopping hooks/captions like a top strategist, not generic best-practice. No em-dashes anywhere. Real content is king, AI is polish only.

## PART 0 · SEVYN'S METHOD (the operating philosophy, outranks generic advice)
- **Clarity is currency.** One clear message beats a scattered feed. If a viewer can't tell in 3 seconds what this is and why they care, they scroll. Every post has ONE point, ONE emotion.
- **Interrupt the scroll.** The first 1 to 3 seconds decide everything. If the opening is boring, the algorithm never pushes it. Stop the scroll first, deliver value second.
- **Be interesting, not perfect.** You don't need lighting or a big following, you need attention. Attention becomes followers, then opportunities. A phone + a real personality + a strong opinion beats polish.
- **Hook → value → CTA.** Every piece: grab attention, give the payoff, ask for one action. Never bury the point ("hey guys, so today...") = dead on arrival.
- **Speak to ONE person.** "New founders building a brand" not "entrepreneurs." Specific feels personal, personal builds community.
- **Withhold, don't broadcast** (Kailin's scarcity brand): a withheld invite ("almost no one knows about this yet") beats performing excitement ("don't miss out"). Insider energy over announcement energy, always, especially on TikTok.

## PART 1 · THE 5 HOOK TYPES (rotate so content never feels repetitive)
Different hooks stop the scroll for different psychological reasons. Name the type when you write a hook.
1. **Curiosity** (information gap): "Nobody tells you this part of ___." "Something happened when I tried ___." Brain wants to close the loop.
2. **Authority/Opinion** (bold stance): "People need to stop doing this." "We need to talk about this." Confident, a little provocative.
3. **Emotion/Story** (conversational, relatable): "Let me tell you something real quick..." warm, direct-to-camera, personal.
4. **Visual pattern-interrupt**: an unexpected first frame that doesn't look like everything else in the feed.
5. **Aspiration/Lifestyle**: a life someone wants to be near, confident presence, strong visual world.

**Bold-Statement formula** (Sevyn's default): open on a bold, slightly incomplete claim that makes them think "wait... what?" Under 12 words, no hype word, in "I/we" voice, on-screen from frame 1 (most watch muted).

## PART 2 · THE 90-SECOND CLIP → POST DECISION (run on every clip, in order)
The first answer narrows the rest.
- **(a) FORMAT:** motion-with-a-moment → **Reel** (IG cinematic cut + TikTok raw cut, one shoot two edits). 3+ stills that sequence → **Carousel**. One raw human beat → **Story** first, repost best frame to feed. One gorgeous still w/ negative space → **single feed post**. Tie-break: if it moves and it's beautiful, it's a Reel; unsure if feed-worthy, it's a Story.
- **(b) PILLAR** (pick ONE, one emotion per post): The Why/Founder · Building It/BTS (the king lane, see Part 5) · The Cause (Habitat, one mention) · Experience Tease · The Movement · Teach/POV/Atlanta-search. If it fits two, pick the one posted least this week.
- **(c) HOOK:** pull from the library (Part 3) or write to spec. Under 12 words, curiosity or stakes, no hype word, "I/we" voice, on screen frame 1.
- **(d) CAPTION:** run the formula (Part 4).
- **(e) CTA:** exactly ONE (Part 4 keyword system). TikTok softer than IG.
- **(f) TEXT OVERLAY:** YES for any Reel / muted-scroll clip (burn hook in frame 1, add a 2nd line ~7s to re-grab). NO for a pure-atmosphere still where caption carries it. Style: clean brass/cream, max 2 lines, 3-5 words/line.

## PART 3 · HOOK LIBRARY (Kailin's voice · under 12 words · a little secret)
**Founder/Why:** "I'm spending my whole summer on a dinner most people will never get into." · "People ask what I actually do. I build the nights Atlanta talks about." · "Atlanta feeds the world every day, in kitchens nobody sees." · "I didn't want to throw a party. I wanted to build a room."
**Building It/BTS:** "This is the roof. Nobody's seen it dressed yet." · "Six weeks out. Here's what we're not showing anyone yet." · "Watch me build the most secret dinner in Atlanta." · "Most people see the night. Almost no one sees this part." · "Day one of building a six-country rooftop from nothing."
**Lifestyle:** "This is the room I live in. Soon I'm letting people in." · "If the planning looks like this, picture the night."
**Show-tease (firewall-safe):** "One night you're not a guest. You're the judge." · "You won't dine. You'll travel, six countries, one roof." · "The night ends on a helipad. That's all I can tell you."
**Atlanta-search (say the keyword out loud + in caption):** "The most unique dinner in Atlanta this summer is one you've never heard of." · "Best date night in Atlanta this summer isn't a restaurant. It's a rooftop."
> Write-your-own spec: open on a withheld secret or a stake. Never "Get ready to" or "Don't miss out." A withheld invite beats "POV: you got invited" for a scarcity brand.

## PART 4 · CAPTION FORMULA + CTA SYSTEM
```
[HOOK LINE]  curiosity/stake, <12 words, matches the on-screen hook
[BODY 2-3 lines]  sensory + specific: what it FEELS like to be there (or to miss it)
[ANCHOR verbatim]  "Six countries. Six cultures. One night Atlanta plates the world."
                   (drop where it lands; NEVER beside the Habitat line)
[ONE CTA]  a single keyword ask or "link in bio"
[HABITAT optional]  only on the Cause post or where natural. One mention, verbatim.
[4-7 hashtags]  Atlanta-search + niche + 1-2 broad
```
**CTA keyword system (one per post):** mystery/top → "Comment WAITLIST and I will add you before it opens up." · founder/why → "Comment STORY and I will send you in." · cause → "Comment CAUSE and I will tell you the rest." · reach Reels → soft "Follow to see how it turns out" / "Link in bio." · final week → "Comment SEATS and I will send you the link."
**Length by phase:** Announce = short/withholding · Build = medium w/ one reveal · Final week = short/urgent · Recap = longer, story-driven.
**Example (Founder):** *I'm spending my whole summer on a dinner most people will never get into. / Atlanta feeds the world every single day, in kitchens nobody sees. I wanted one night that pulls those hands into the middle of the room. This is what I've been quietly building. / Six countries. Six cultures. One night Atlanta plates the world. / Comment STORY and I will send you in.*

## PART 5 · THE BTS / "BUILDING IT" PILLAR (the cult-marketing engine, over-deliver here)
This is the neglected lane that makes a page feel like a person, not a flyer ("come build this with me"). Kailin has tons of real footage here (passport-making, favor tins, venue scouts, late-night assembly). Treat it as the king lane.
1. **Access, not activity.** Don't film "here's me setting up." Open on a withheld reveal: "Almost no one sees this part." The viewer should feel chosen, let into a door.
2. **Hold the grade even on raw footage.** Warm highlights, lifted shadows, slight desaturation, brass/cream/walnut. Raw + right grade = intentional. Pretty + cold HDR = flyer.
3. **One quiet moment beats five busy ones.** A steady 4-second hold of a hand on the passport, a candle catching, beats a fast montage. Restraint is the brand. Jacquemus, not girlboss.
4. **Contrast + time create the hook.** "The roof at 6pm vs what you'll walk into." "Day one vs now." People follow a build, run a recurring "day X of building it" series.
5. **Your face, your voice, your standard.** The most magnetic BTS is you saying ONE true thing about why it has to be this good, over the footage. State the belief, not the task: "I build the rooms Atlanta talks about, and this is the standard."

## PART 6 · NON-NEGOTIABLES (every post passes these)
- **Personal-first routing:** a tie goes to the personal page; Plated earns a clip only with clear event/brand fit. Founder-in-public builds the audience Plated converts.
- **Anchor (verbatim, never reworded):** "Six countries. Six cultures. One night Atlanta plates the world."
- **HARD-BLOCK words (strip before output):** world-class · best-in-class · premier · elevate/elevated · redefine · reimagine · delight · curated · bespoke · seamless · culinary journey · sensory experience · unforgettable. Also kill: three-adjective stacks, emoji-as-punctuation, "we're thrilled to announce."
- **NO em-dashes or en-dashes, ever.** Comma, period, or middle-dot · only.
- **Habitat:** one mention per post max, never beside the anchor. Verbatim: "10% of every ticket goes to Habitat for Humanity Atlanta."
- **Firewall (one-line test before anything ships):** "Does this reveal that July 30 is launching a chef-accelerator business?" If yes, cut it. Public-safe = "a guest-voted winner crowned live." NEVER name the Collective, cohort, accelerator, ceremony, or lexicon publicly. Tease the SHOW, not the engine.
- **Real content is king.** Real footage is the hero; AI is motion/composite/b-roll polish only, never the source. Fully-AI = slop, pull it.
- **Public-safe facts:** Plated Circuit Vol I · Thu July 30 · Ventanas rooftop · 200 seats · GA $175 / Passport $215 / VIP $275 · France · Morocco · Mexico · India · Italy · USA finale · you're a judge, you vote, a winner crowned live · helipad finale · private-elevator VIP arrival · craft cocktails + a zero-proof version of each.

## When judging a clip you can't fully see
You often get ONE still from a video, no audio. Do NOT invent dialogue, on-screen text, or a reveal you can't see in the frame. If a hook depends on unseen footage, mark it an assumption and route it to the shotlist, do not assert it. Creator notes (if provided) are ground truth about who/what is in frame, never override them with a guess.
