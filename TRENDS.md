# Trend & Timing Desk

Native virality is trend + timing + audio. A static skill knows none of it, so this stage FETCHES it. Run for every clip headed to TikTok/Reels (and any timely personal post).

## Per clip, attach
1. **Candidate audio (verify in-app)** · use WebSearch for currently-circulating TikTok/Reels sounds that fit the clip's mood, and propose 2 to 3 named candidates. BE HONEST: WebSearch cannot see the live TikTok audio chart, so these are CANDIDATES to confirm in the app's own "trending" panel before posting, never asserted as today's top sound. If you cannot find a credible current candidate, say "use a trending sound from the app's panel that matches {mood}" rather than inventing a track name. Never fabricate a song title.
2. **Format/trend it can ride** · the current meme, transition, or format the footage fits.
3. **Posting window** · best time for Kailin's audience (Atlanta, her follower active hours). Default guidance if unknown: weekday late-morning and evening, but prefer her own analytics if available.
4. **Timeliness flag** · `evergreen` / `post within 7 days` / `post within 48h, hot`.

## Timeliness drives order, not quality score
Time-sensitive cultural moments jump to the FRONT of the post order. The FIFA Fan Fest footage is a live moment with a built-in audience and searchable interest. It must be flagged `post within 48h` and lead the day, not get buried under a higher-craft but evergreen clip.

## Honesty
Trend reads are only as good as the live search at run time. State the date of the trend check. If WebSearch is unavailable, say so and route audio selection to Kailin rather than guessing a stale sound.

## Don't
- Don't force a trending sound onto a clip whose mood it fights.
- Don't recommend a peaked/declining audio when a rising one fits.
- Don't sacrifice the authenticity premium for a trend, a real confessional with no trending sound can still be the right call on personal.
